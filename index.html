At a Glance

A bridge between
science and care

Dr. Archana Basu portfolio of clinical programs and research bridges the gap between population science and individual clinical care. Trained at Michigan State University (PhD, Clinical Psychology), she completed postdoctoral fellowships at Cornell/NewYork-Presbyterian, Columbia University Medical Center (developmental neuroscience), and Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health (epidemiology), before joining the Harvard/MGH faculty in 2016.

She directs the Pediatric Population Health in Action program, which examines how early trauma, particularly in the perinatal and early childhood periods, shapes children's behavioral, biological, and physical health outcomes. She investigates both the mechanisms of harm (maternal stress biology, DNA methylation, caregiving sensitivity) and the pathways to resilience (parent-child relationships, trauma-informed interventions, integrated primary care).

At MGH, she co-founded and co-directs the Integrated Primary Care (IPC) Program, which embeds psychologists and social workers in primary care pediatric and adolescent clinics — delivering more than 1,000 patient consultations since 2021. She is also Principal Investigator in the Broad Trauma Initiative's pediatric work stream at the Broad Institute. This transformational grant aims to scale trauma-informed interventions in MGH's pediatric care system.

Internationally, Dr. Basu is a lead investigator on the PrOMIS cohort — a landmark longitudinal birth cohort study of over 3,300 low-income Peruvian women and their children, now followed through middle childhood.

Research Focus Areas

Key Themes in Dr. Basu's Work

01
Intergenerational Trauma & Child Development
The central question driving Dr. Basu's research program: how does a mother's lifetime history of trauma shape her child's behavioral and biological development — and what mechanisms explain this link?
  • NICHD R01 examining chronicity and timing of maternal trauma and 3-year-olds' behavioral health (DNA methylation and caregiving sensitivity as mechanisms)
  • PrOMIS cohort: prenatal cortisone dysregulation predicts school-age behavioral problems, especially in girls (Künzel et al., 2026)
  • Maternal trauma history and child behavior outcomes in a Peruvian mother-child cohort (Regan et al., 2025)
Epigenetics DNA Methylation HPA Axis Birth Cohort
02
Trauma-Informed Pediatric Population Health
Dr. Basu leads a major program to embed trauma-informed, transdiagnostic mental health care into pediatric primary and specialty care at MGH/MGB — designed to reach children and families at population scale.
  • Co-founder and co-director of the MGH Integrated Primary Care (IPC) Program: psychologists embedded in primary care pediatric clinics
  • Principal Investigator: Broad Institute grant (2024–2029) to scale trauma-informed group interventions for children ages 2–12
  • Pilot study of tiered trauma-informed model across primary and secondary care (Mikesell et al., 2025)
  • Co-developer of DBT-Elementary (DBT-E), a novel adapted intervention under pragmatic trial
TIC Integrated Care Implementation Science DBT-E
03
Childhood Adversity & Physical Health
A foundational thread in Dr. Basu's work: understanding why children exposed to trauma face elevated risks for chronic physical conditions across the lifespan — and how to break that chain.
  • Childhood maltreatment and risk for cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes in adults (Basu et al., 2017)
  • Childhood violence exposure and chronic conditions in a national US adolescent sample (McLaughlin, Basu et al., 2016)
  • Biological assessments pre/post intervention to track physiological effects of psychotherapy
ACEs Cardiometabolic Risk Physical Health Biomarkers
04
Perinatal & Maternal Mental Health
Pregnancy and early parenthood are critical windows for identifying and treating trauma. Dr. Basu's research spans populations from Lima, Peru to global COVID-19 cohorts, examining PTSD, depression, and prenatal stress.
  • PrOMIS cohort: 87.8% lifetime trauma prevalence; PTSD rates 4× the US average in pregnant Peruvian women (Gelaye et al., 2017; Levey et al., 2018)
  • Cross-national COVID-19 perinatal mental health study across multiple countries (Basu et al., 2021)
  • Sleep as a protective factor for depression and anxiety in the perinatal period using novel causal analysis (Basu et al., 2024)
  • Pandemic stress and child behavioral outcomes in Peru (Blevins et al., 2025)
Perinatal Health PTSD Global Health COVID-19
05
Resilience, Protective Factors & Parenting
Rather than cataloguing risk alone, Dr. Basu's work actively identifies what protects children from harm — particularly the quality of the parent-child relationship — and turns those insights into intervention targets.
  • Maternal caregiving sensitivity as a mechanism linking trauma history to child outcomes (NICHD R01)
  • Protective factors for family wellbeing during COVID-19 in child psychiatric settings (Urban, Friedman, Basu et al., 2022)
  • Parent advisory board model integrated into trauma-informed clinical research (presented 2024–2025)
  • Perinatal parenting and sleep medicine program development (Saltonstall Foundation)
Parenting Resilience Parent-Child Relationship CBPR
06
Intimate Partner & Family Violence
From her doctoral work through her current research, Dr. Basu has studied the biological and psychological sequelae of intimate partner violence — in women who experience it and the children who witness it.
  • Cortisol functioning and HPA axis dysregulation in women exposed to IPV (Basu et al., 2013; Blue Cross Blue Shield award)
  • Longitudinal group intervention for women and children exposed to domestic violence (Basu et al., 2009)
  • IPV and financial adversity as predictors of familial homelessness in the ALSPAC cohort (Chan, Sarvet, Basu et al., 2021)
  • Clinical programming: teen healthy relationships and IPV awareness for adolescents
IPV Cortisol / HPA Family Violence Group Intervention

Post-Traumatic Stress Across the Lifespan
Drawing on large epidemiological samples and the AURORA Study — a major NIMH-funded longitudinal cohort of trauma survivors — Dr. Basu examines PTSD trajectories, comorbidities, and risk factors across populations.
  • AURORA Study: multimodal, longitudinal NIMH cohort of trauma survivors presenting to emergency departments (McLean, Basu et al., 2020)
  • Psychiatric comorbidity following stress disorder diagnoses in Danish school-aged children (Basu et al., 2020)
  • Network analysis of culturally specific trauma responses in Vietnam (Trang, Basu et al., 2025)
  • Systematic review of psychotherapy interventions for childhood traumatic stress (2025)

Clinical Practice & Program Leadership

Where Research Meets Lived Experience

Dr. Basu is a practicing clinical psychologist and holds active licenses in Massachusetts and New York. She works with children, adolescents, young adults, and their families to support their coping and resilience in the context of adversities, trauma, serious medical illnesses, grief, and bereavement.

Beyond individual care, she has led systemic clinical program development at MGH: as a founding co-director of the Integrated Primary Care (IPC) program, building trauma-informed, family-centered, and group-based parenting intervention teams, and co-developing the ALS Parenting At a Challenging Time (PACT) Program, which supports ALS patients who are parents/grandparents. Moreover, she also conducts clinical research examining trauma-informed transdiagnostic interventions and is a co-developer of Dialectical Behavior Therapy-Elementary (DBT-E) intervention for children (ages 7–12) and their parents/caregivers, which aims to improve children's emotional behavioral regulation skills and parents' efficacy as "emotion coaches." Finally, Dr. Basu is engaged in cross-sector collaboratives for trauma-informed public health approaches to address upstream social determinants of mental health across the lifecourse.

  • ✓ Childhood Trauma & PTSD: Assessment and trauma-focused psychotherapy (TF-CBT) for children and adolescents with histories of physical, emotional, or sexual abuse; domestic violence exposure.
  • ✓ Parenting & Early Childhood: Group and individual interventions for parents and families with children ages 2–12, including DBT-Elementary and Integrated Primary Care brief consults.
  • ✓ Perinatal Mental Health: Support for pregnant individuals and new parents navigating depression, anxiety, PTSD, and the challenges of the transition to parenthood.
  • ✓ Chronic & Terminal Illness: Psychological support for parents and families of patients with ALS and other life-threatening conditions through the MGH Healey Center PACT Program (Parenting At a Challenging Time).
  • ✓ Grief & Bereavement: Clinical care for children and families navigating significant loss, including death of a caregiver.
  • ✓ Teen Relationships & IPV Prevention: Psychoeducation and clinical support for adolescents on healthy relationships and recognition of intimate partner violence.

Integrated Primary Care (IPC) Program

Co-directed with Dr. Nancy Rotter in 2021, IPC embeds psychologists and social workers in MGH primary care pediatric and adolescent medicine clinics to screen, evaluate, triage, and deliver time-limited evidence-based interventions for children from birth through age 21. The program has delivered over 1,000 patient consultations since launch.

DBT-Elementary (DBT-E)

DBT-Elementary (DBT-E) is a 12-week, trauma-informed, family-based intervention for children ages 7–12 with emerging emotional or behavioral dysregulation. It is currently under pragmatic trial. Led by Makiko Watanabe, PhD. Co-Developers: Makiko Watanabe, PhD · Christina Dolce, PsyD · Archana Basu, PhD (2023).

Parenting Young Children

A 12-week, trauma-informed, group-based intervention for parents and caregivers of children ages 2–6 with emerging emotional dysregulation, led by Dana Allswede, PhD. The program builds developmental understanding, concrete behavior navigation strategies, parenting self-awareness, and co-regulation skills. A Spanish-language version of this intervention was also piloted.

The Daniella Lipper ALS Parenting At a Challenging Time (PACT) Program

Co-developed with Drs. Cindy Moore and Paula Rausch, this program provides guidance to support parents and grandparents in addressing the ALS-related challenges faced by their children and adolescents such as coping with a loved ones' ALS-related progression, anticipatory grief, and bereavement. The ALS-PACT program offers a range of psychoeducational resources to children and families and clinicians.

Research Training, Clinical Supervision, & Mentorship

Building the Next Generation of Scholars, Clinical Researchers, & Practitioners

Mentorship is central to Dr. Basu's work. She has trained over 20 mentees at the undergraduate, master's, doctoral, postdoctoral, and faculty levels — receiving multiple institutional nominations and awards for exceptional mentorship, with particular recognition for mentoring women faculty and trainees. She co-founded the Anti-Racism and Diversity Working Group in the MGH Division of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry and served on the Diversity, Inclusion, and Belonging committee at Harvard Chan.

Dr. Basu has supervised psychology interns, fellows, staff psychologists, and clinical social workers continuously since 2011. She leads or co-leads multiple clinical programs at MassGeneral Hospital.
